How Heat Exchangers Work

Before selecting materials, it is important to understand the basic workings of a heat exchanger.

The purpose of a heat exchanger is to extract heat from a hot process fluid and transfer it to a benign fluid such as water.

One fluid flows through the tubes, the other fluid flows through the shell outside the tubes.

A shell and tube heat exchanger is constructed of tubes that are attached on each end by a plate that the tubes goes through called a tube sheet.
The internal area where the tubes are located is enclosed by a shell or enclosure.
